Who Am I?

I embody three fundamental qualities: resilience, efficacy, and determination. Presently, I am a first-year undergraduate student enrolled at Far Eastern University Alabang, persistently pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Information Technology with a specialization in Web and Mobile Applications (BSIT-WMA).

What Is Unique About Me?

I believe in infusing enjoyment into my work, recognizing that my proficiency and the quality of my output thrive when I am genuinely engaged. However, this does not diminish the seriousness with which I approach your requests. On the contrary, I am committed to crafting unique solutions imbued with my creative perspective. Should the need arise, I am fully capable of delivering results that adhere to corporate standards. My journey into programming has largely been self-directed, driven by a desire to contribute meaningfully to the technology industry and, admittedly, to secure financial stability. Fortunately, my personal interests harmonize with IT concepts and creative endeavors, fortuitously aligning with my career path.

What Are My Thoughts On My Career Choice?

I wish to be a prominent part of the computer studies field, a discipline I am passionate about and believe has the most potential and capability for advancing humanity in the 21st century. Through this program, I will work relentlessly to succeed and make a name for myself once I enter the industry.

What Is My Dream Job?

As IT and CS-related fields are extensive in their range of specializations, I am still exploring possibilities of what I could specialize in for my future career. But, considering my current course and plans, I intend to pursue a high position at an established international tech company as a distinguished senior or leading developer, which I consider my dream job.

Where Do I See Yourself In 10 Years?

n 10 years, I would have graduated from FEU Alabang with a Bachelor's Degree in Information Technology. If I ever decide to continue pursuing a Master's Degree in Computer Science from another college or university, I would have graduated with that as well. By then, I would have secured a solid job position in the tech industry as a developer or in another IT/CS-related role performing at a similarly high level. My career portfolio would be complete with renowned certifications related to my specialization, impressive IT/CS-related projects that I formulated by myself or with a group, academic awards showcasing resilience and exceptional performance, and proof of my excellence as a willing and diligent worker. I would have involved myself in small-scale and large projects recognized by companies and everyday people. Finally, I would continue to enhance my skills and abilities to secure higher positions and to improve the demand for my expertise in the tech industry.
